The lower leaves are turning yellow. Is something wrong?
Based on your Monstera's care profile, yellowing lower leaves can indicate overwatering. Your current watering interval is every 10 days, which is typical for Monstera — but several factors might warrant adjustment:

• Season: In winter, most Monsteras need less frequent watering (every 12-14 days)
• Soil check: Let the top 2-3 inches of soil dry out between waterings
• Drainage: Ensure the pot has drainage holes and water isn't pooling

Try extending your interval to 12-14 days and see if the yellowing stops. If new yellow leaves appear on top, that could indicate a different issue like low light or nutrient deficiency.
Should I cut off the yellow leaves?
Yes, you can safely remove them! Yellow leaves won't recover, and removing them lets your Monstera redirect energy to healthy growth.

Use clean, sharp scissors and cut at the base of the leaf stem where it meets the main stalk. This is essentially a light pruning — you might want to log it in your care journal.
